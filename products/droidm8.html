Install Droid M8 for Mac

Paste this in Terminal (Applications → Utilities, or Spotlight → “Terminal”) and press Return. It downloads the app, verifies its checksum, installs it to /Applications, and launches it.

curl -fsSL https://tech-m8.solutions/downloads/droidm8/install.sh | sh

No Gatekeeper warning: a curl download isn’t quarantined the way a browser download is, so the app — though not signed with an Apple Developer ID — launches without the “Apple could not verify…” block.

Setup walkthrough

Pair the two apps in a few minutes. Screenshots from the real app.

  1. 1

    Install both apps

    Grab the APK on your Android phone and install the Mac app with the one-line Terminal command below. The Android app needs Android 9+. The Mac app needs macOS 13+ and runs from the menu bar.

    Jump to downloads
  2. 2

    Open Droid M8 on your Mac

    First launch opens a window with three tabs. Go to Devices — you'll see a 6-digit pairing PIN. Keep this window open; the phone needs that number next.

  3. 3

    Find the app from your menu bar

    Droid M8 lives in the menu bar. Click the icon any time to reopen the window, send the clipboard, or jump to recent items. The pairing PIN also shows under Clipboard in the main menu.

  4. 4

    Pair from your phone

    Open Droid M8 on your phone, tap Settings (bottom-right), then the Devices tab. Type in the PIN from your Mac. Status flips to Connected once the two apps agree on the PIN.

  5. 5

    Grant Android permissions

    Tap Notifications in the bottom nav, then the Settings sub-tab, then the Permissions chip. Tap each row — Read Notifications, Send Notification, DND policy access — and approve in Android system settings.

  6. 6

    Grant Full Disk Access on the Mac

    For Mac → Phone notification mirroring, the Mac app needs Full Disk Access (it reads macOS's notification database). Open the Notifications tab on the Mac → click "Open Full Disk Access settings" → toggle Droid M8 on.

  7. 7

    You're paired — copy & paste across devices

    Copy text on either device — it shows up on the other within a second, end-to-end encrypted with your PIN. The Clipboard tab on either side keeps a history you can re-copy or resend.

  8. 8

    Bonus: DND-bypass rules

    Add a rule under Notifications → DND Bypass to make specific apps + keywords re-alert your phone even when Do Not Disturb is on. Pick the app, add keywords, pick a sound.

  9. 9

    Bonus: suppress noisy apps + back up rules

    Suppress quietly cancels notifications from chosen apps and logs them to history. Backup writes your rules to a JSON file on Google Drive so reinstalling doesn't wipe them.
